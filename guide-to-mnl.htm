Guide to Manila

  1. Direct flight to PH

    1. PAL There's a direct flight from SF and LA to MNL via PAL. Flight takes 12-15 hours non-stop.

  2. Apps to install

    1. Grab (transport and even payment)

      1. Please install Grab on your phone. It's an omni-app that is primarily used to book transport (like Uber), food delivery (like UberEats, DoorDash), and even a digital wallet that can pay locally through QR.

  3. Money situation

    1. At Least 200 usd in cash as pesos

      1. 200 USD is 10,000 PHP+ and will be able to tide you over in many things that require only cash.

    2. Majority (80%) of anything that needs transactions are capable of taking payment through QR (see Grab money). The most ubiquitous choices are Gcash and Maya but both need a local sim card to set-up, so we recommend Grab instead!

    3. A lot of stores can take credit cards ofcourse, but there will be circumstances where their terminal isn't working. By then, QR code payment and/or having cash comes handy.

  4. Weather

    1. It's hot.

    2. It can rain.

    3. Both cases, a foldable umbrella comes in handy.

  5. Location info

    1. BGC – A walkable, safe Central Business District in Taguig. It's a privately developed estate. 

    2. BGC is locally known as a city that “exists in a bubble” because it doesn't truly reflect the entire country. While everyone we know who visits the Philippines for the first time is surprised and amazed at how “modern, safe, and convenient” it is, this perception can change when you decide to leave the boundaries of BGC. However, we highly suggest that you remain vigilant with your belongings still.

      1. Hospital

        1. Should you have an emergency, BGC has St Luke's Global City - one of the best hospitals in the country.
